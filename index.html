OUR STORY

From Ulster to the American South

The McCluney story begins in November 1772 when William McCluney emigrated from County Antrim, Ireland, securing a land grant in Chester County, South Carolina. Through nine documented generations — marked by the rare hereditary naming of Byron across three generations — the family has built a legacy as backcountry farmers, blacksmiths, doctors, and veterans across the South.

This digital family tree was created by Byron Patrick McCluney (BYRON #3) as a living resource for all McCluneys. It preserves verified research, DNA community connections (Early Alabama/Mississippi/Louisiana/East Texas settlers), and the stories of those who came before us — from Revolutionary War service to Vietnam.

How to Contribute

The easiest way to add or correct information is to edit the js/data.js file and open a Pull Request on GitHub. You can also email verified details, photos, or documents.

What we especially need:
  • Birth, marriage, and death records or certificates
  • Old family photos with names, dates, and locations
  • Stories, oral histories, and letters
  • DNA matches and GEDCOM files
  • Taylor line details and maternal great-grandmother's maiden name
