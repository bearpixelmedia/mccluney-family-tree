OUR STORY

From Ulster to the American South

The McCluney story begins in November 1772 when William McCluney emigrated from County Antrim, Ireland, securing a land grant in Chester County, South Carolina. Through nine documented generations — marked by the rare hereditary naming of Byron across three generations — the family has built a legacy as backcountry farmers, blacksmiths, doctors, and veterans across the South.

This digital family tree was created by Byron Patrick McCluney (BYRON #3) as a living resource for all McCluneys. It preserves verified research, DNA community connections (Early Alabama/Mississippi/Louisiana/East Texas settlers), and the stories of those who came before us — from Revolutionary War service to Vietnam. Now with tools to actively discover more from public sources.

How to Contribute & Research

1. Use the Research Hub above to search public sources (WikiTree, Find A Grave, FamilySearch).

2. Run the local Python crawler script (in scripts/) to automatically discover profiles.

3. Edit js/data.js to add verified people and relationships, then open a Pull Request on GitHub.

What we especially need:
  • Verified birth/marriage/death records
  • Old family photos with identifications
  • Stories and oral histories
  • DNA matches and additional branches (Taylor/Whatley lines)
